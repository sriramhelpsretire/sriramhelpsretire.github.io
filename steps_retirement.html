5 steps in retirement

The process of retirement planning involves assessing current finances, setting a desired lifestyle goal, calculating the necessary target portfolio, and implementing an investment strategy to achieve it.

Define Retirement Goals and Timeline

Identify desired lifestyle: Consider where you will live, travel plans, and anticipated expenses.

Estimate retirement age: Determine when you can afford to stop working and when to claim Social Security.

Assess Financial Standing

Calculate net worth: List all assets and debts.

Estimate costs: Develop a budget that accounts for changes like higher healthcare costs.

Develop a Savings and Investment Strategy

Maximize contributions: Utilize tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs.

Choose investments: Align investments with your risk tolerance.

Use an HSA: Consider a Health Savings Account for future medical expenses.

Plan for Income and Risks

Create a withdrawal plan: Use strategies like the 4% rule to determine safe spending.

Plan for healthcare: Evaluate Medicare options and long-term care insurance.

Build a buffer: Factor in inflation and potential market downturns.

Review and Refine

Reassess periodically: Review your investments at least annually.

Adjust as needed: As you approach retirement, shift toward more conservative investments.
