Medicare

The Four Parts of Medicare
Part A (Hospital Insurance): Covers inpatient hospital stays, care in a skilled nursing facility, hospice care, and some home health care.
Part B (Medical Insurance): Covers certain doctors' services, outpatient care, medical supplies, and preventive services.
Part C (Medicare Advantage): An all-in-one alternative to Original Medicare, offered by private companies approved by Medicare. These "bundled" plans include Part A, Part B, and usually Part D.
Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage): Helps cover the cost of prescription drugs (including many recommended shots or vaccines).
Medigap: Private insurance, known as Medicare Supplement Insurance, can be purchased to help pay out-of-pocket costs in Original Medicare.

Medicare application should generally start during your 7-month Initial Enrollment Period (IEP), which begins 3 months before your 65th birthday month and ends 3 months after.
