What is Retirement?

A New Phase, A New Purpose

The Definition of Retirement

Retirement is the phase of life when an individual stops working full-time, typically due to age, health, or personal choice. It represents a transition from a lifestyle supported by a steady paycheck to one supported by accumulated savings, investments, or pensions. While historically often starting around age 65, retirement has become increasingly flexible, with options to retire earlier or later based on financial readiness and lifestyle goals.

Key Aspects of a Modern Retirement

  • Financial Shift: Relying on accumulated assets (e.g., 401k plans, Social Security) rather than a monthly salary.
  • Life Stage Transition: Moving from a structured career to a newly defined, often self-directed, daily routine.
  • Work Flexibility: No longer strictly meaning "stopping all work," but perhaps moving to part-time, consulting, or passion-driven careers.
  • Dynamic Phase: It is not a static finish line, but a dynamic 30+ year journey that requires active planning.
