6 Areas of Comprehensive Financial Planning

What are the 6 areas of comprehensive financial planning?

The six core areas of financial planning include financial position (cash flow/debt), protection planning (insurance), investment planning, tax planning, retirement planning, and estate planning.

1. Financial Position/Cash Flow Management

  • Cash flow / budgeting
  • Debt management and emergency reserves planning

2. Protection Planning (Risk Management)

  • Life, Disability, and Long-term care insurance
  • Employee benefits

3. Investment Planning

  • Portfolio management and institutional level management
  • Evaluating risk tolerance and time horizon

4. Tax Planning

  • Company retirement plans and personalization
  • Traditional, Roth, and Rollover IRAs

5. Retirement Planning

  • Distribution planning and income identification
  • Plan for "sequence-of-returns risk"

6. Estate Planning

  • Wealth transfer and business succession
  • Co-ordination of insurance and estate documents

Each area is critical and should be planned alongside others, not in silos.
