Financial Planning

What are the 6 areas of comprehensive financial planning?

The six core areas of financial planning include financial position (cash flow/debt), protection planning (insurance), investment planning, tax planning, retirement planning, and estate planning. These areas provide a comprehensive strategy to manage assets, mitigate risks, and secure long-term financial stability and growth.

1.  Financial Position/Cash Flow Management: Analyzing net worth (assets vs. debt), budgeting, and managing cash reserves to understand current financial health.

  • Cash flow / budgeting
  • Debt management and emergency reserves planning
  • Financial ratios

2. Protection Planning (Risk Management): Safeguarding against financial loss using life, disability, and long-term care insurance, as well as managing personal liability risks.

  • Life insurance
  • Disability Insurance
  • Long-term care insurance
  • Employee benefits

3. Investment Planning: Strategizing asset allocation, diversification, and investment selection to meet goals while considering risk tolerance.

  • Portfolio management
  • Personalized investment programs
  • Platforms utilizing institutional level management
  • Evaluating risk tolerance and time horizon

4. Tax Planning: Implementing strategies to minimize tax liability through tax-deferred investing and understanding tax implications of income and investments.

  • Implementation and development of company sponsored retirement plans
  • Personalization of plan(s)
  • Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, Rollover IRA
  • 529 college savings plans — Employee stock option planning and analysis

5. Retirement Planning: Setting goals for retirement income and developing strategies using tools like 401(k)s, IRAs, and Social Security to sustain a lifestyle.

  • Distribution planning
  • Identification of income needs
  • Plan for "sequence-of-returns risk"

6. Estate Planning: Organizing the transfer of assets, creating wills, establishing trusts, and setting up guardianship to manage legacies.

  • Wealth transfer
  • Business succession
  • Charitable planning
  • Co-ordination of insurance policies and estate planning documents

Each of the above 6 areas is critical and is to be planned alongside and not in silos. Decisions on one area could affect or should consider other areas as well.
