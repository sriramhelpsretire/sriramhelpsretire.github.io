Financial Planning

What are the 6 areas of comprehensive financial planning?

The six core areas of financial planning include financial position (cash flow/debt), protection planning (insurance), investment planning, tax planning, retirement planning, and estate planning. These areas provide a comprehensive strategy to manage assets, mitigate risks, and secure long-term financial stability and growth.
01.

Financial Position

Analyzing net worth (assets vs. debt), budgeting, and managing cash reserves.

  • Cash flow / budgeting
  • Debt management and emergency reserves planning
  • Financial ratios
02.

Protection Planning

Safeguarding against financial loss using insurance and managing personal liability.

  • Life insurance / Disability Insurance
  • Long-term care insurance
  • Employee benefits
03.

Investment Planning

Strategizing asset allocation and diversification to meet goals based on risk tolerance.

  • Portfolio management
  • Personalized investment programs
  • Evaluating risk tolerance and time horizon
04.

Tax Planning

Implementing strategies to minimize tax liability through tax-deferred investing.

  • Company sponsored retirement plans
  • Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, Rollover IRA
  • 529 college savings / Stock option planning
05.

Retirement Planning

Setting goals for retirement income using tools like 401(k)s, IRAs, and Social Security.

  • Distribution planning
  • Identification of income needs
  • Plan for "sequence-of-returns risk"
06.

Estate Planning

Organizing asset transfer, creating wills, and establishing trusts to manage legacies.

  • Wealth transfer / Business succession
  • Charitable planning
  • Coordination of insurance and estate documents
Each of the above 6 areas is critical and is to be planned alongside and not in silos. Decisions on one area could affect or should consider other areas as well.
